Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Prophet Muhammad (570–632 CE), the final messenger of Islam, was born in Mecca. Through divine revelation, he brought the message of monotheism, social justice, and compassion. The Qur’an, revealed to him over 23 years, remains the holy book of Islam. He unified Arabia under the message of Islam and emphasized ethics, knowledge, and mercy. He is considered the "Seal of the Prophets."

Born: 570 CE, Mecca
Died: 632 CE, Medina
Buried: Al-Masjid an-Nabawi, Medina

Fatimah al-Zahra (سلام الله علیها)

Fatimah al-Zahra (605–632 CE), daughter of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and wife of Imam Ali (ع), is revered for her piety, wisdom, and role in defending the Prophet's message after his death. She is the mother of Hasan and Husayn and is considered a role model for Muslim women. Her sermons and actions after the Prophet's demise remain deeply respected and studied in Islamic history.

Born: 605 CE, Mecca
Died: 632 CE, Medina
Buried: Unknown, Medina

Imam Ali (ع)

Ali ibn Abi Talib (c. 600 – 661 CE) was the cousin and son-in-law of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He was the first male to accept Islam and is revered as the first Imam in Shia Islam and the fourth Caliph in Sunni tradition. Known for his wisdom, bravery, and justice, he played a key role in early Islamic battles and governance. His sermons and sayings are preserved in the famous work Nahj al-Balagha.

Born: circa 600 CE, inside the Kaaba, Mecca
Died: 661 CE, Kufa
Buried: Najaf, Iraq

Imam Hasan (ع)

Imam Hasan ibn Ali (c. 625 – 670 CE) was the eldest grandson of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and the second Imam in Shia Islam. Known for his deep knowledge and generosity, he became Imam after his father Ali (ع) and later entered a peace treaty with Muawiyah to prevent civil war within the Muslim community. His patience and wisdom are highly praised in Islamic history.

Born: 625 CE, Medina
Died: 670 CE, allegedly poisoned
Buried: Jannat al-Baqi, Medina

Imam Husayn (ع)

Imam Husayn ibn Ali (626 – 680 CE) was the younger grandson of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and the third Imam in Shia Islam. He is most known for his courageous stand against tyranny at the Battle of Karbala in 680 CE, where he and his companions were martyred. His sacrifice is honored annually on Ashura. He symbolizes resistance, justice, and faith.

Born: 626 CE, Medina
Martyred: 680 CE, Karbala
Buried: Karbala, Iraq

Imam Zayn al-Abidin (ع)

Imam Ali ibn Husayn, also known as Zayn al-Abidin or Imam Sajjad (c. 658 – 713 CE), was the son of Imam Husayn (ع) and the fourth Imam in Shia Islam. He survived the Battle of Karbala and is best known for his spiritual teachings and supplications, compiled in the renowned book Al-Sahifa al-Sajjadiyya. He led the community during a time of political turmoil with wisdom and piety.

Born: c. 658 CE, Medina
Died: 713 CE, allegedly poisoned
Buried: Jannat al-Baqi, Medina

Imam Muhammad al-Baqir (ع)

Imam Muhammad al-Baqir (676 – 732 CE) was the fifth Imam in Shia Islam and the son of Imam Zayn al-Abidin (ع). His title "al-Baqir" means "the one who splits knowledge open," signifying his deep expertise in Islamic sciences, jurisprudence, and Quranic interpretation. He established a strong base of scholarly learning in Medina that laid the foundation for future generations of Islamic thought.

Born: 676 CE, Medina
Died: 732 CE, allegedly poisoned
Buried: Jannat al-Baqi, Medina

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (ع)

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (702 – 765 CE) was the sixth Imam in Shia Islam and one of the most influential figures in Islamic jurisprudence. He founded the Ja'fari school of thought and taught many prominent scholars, including Abu Hanifa and Malik ibn Anas. He contributed immensely to theology, ethics, and science, fostering a golden age of Islamic intellectualism during the Umayyad and Abbasid periods.

Born: 702 CE, Medina
Died: 765 CE, allegedly poisoned
Buried: Jannat al-Baqi, Medina

Imam Musa al-Kazim (ع)

Imam Musa al-Kazim (745 – 799 CE) was the seventh Imam in Shia Islam. Known as "al-Kazim" meaning "the one who swallows his anger," he exemplified patience and perseverance during severe persecution under the Abbasid caliphs. Much of his life was spent under surveillance or in prison. Despite restrictions, he nurtured many disciples and defended the rights of the Ahl al-Bayt through quiet endurance.

Born: 745 CE, Medina
Died: 799 CE, Baghdad (martyrdom in prison)
Buried: Kadhimayn, Iraq

Imam Ali al-Ridha (ع)

Imam Ali al-Ridha (766 – 818 CE) was the eighth Imam in Shia Islam. Known for his eloquence and deep knowledge, he was invited by the Abbasid Caliph al-Ma'mun to be his successor, a move seen as politically motivated. Imam Ridha engaged in several public theological debates and dialogues with scholars of different faiths. He is revered for his wisdom, diplomacy, and piety.

Born: 766 CE, Medina
Died: 818 CE, poisoned in Tus
Buried: Mashhad, Iran

Imam Muhammad al-Taqi (ع)

Imam Muhammad al-Taqi (810 – 835 CE), also known as Imam al-Jawad, was the ninth Imam in Shia Islam. He assumed the role of Imam at a young age and was known for his unmatched knowledge, wisdom, and piety. Despite facing political pressures from the Abbasid caliphs, he held several theological debates and guided his followers through correspondence and scholarship.

Born: 810 CE, Medina
Died: 835 CE, Baghdad (allegedly poisoned)
Buried: Kadhimayn, Iraq

Imam Ali al-Hadi (ع)

Imam Ali al-Hadi (828 – 868 CE), also known as Imam al-Naqi, was the tenth Imam in Shia Islam. He was recognized for his calm leadership and dedication to spreading religious knowledge during a time of intense political surveillance. Despite being confined in Samarra by the Abbasid regime, he maintained communication with his followers and addressed theological issues through letters.

Born: 828 CE, Medina
Died: 868 CE, Samarra (allegedly poisoned)
Buried: Al-Askari Shrine, Samarra, Iraq

Imam Hasan al-Askari (ع)

Imam Hasan al-Askari (846 – 874 CE) was the eleventh Imam in Shia Islam. He lived under house arrest in Samarra by the Abbasid caliphs and was closely monitored throughout his life. Despite these restrictions, he continued to guide his followers through letters and trusted representatives. He is the father of the awaited twelfth Imam, Imam al-Mahdi (عج).

Born: 846 CE, Medina
Died: 874 CE, Samarra (allegedly poisoned)
Buried: Al-Askari Shrine, Samarra, Iraq

Imam Muhammad al-Mahdi (عج)

Imam Muhammad ibn Hasan al-Mahdi (born 869 CE) is the twelfth and final Imam in Twelver Shia Islam. Believed to be in occultation (ghaybah), he is known as the awaited savior who will reappear to establish global justice and peace. His occultation is divided into two periods: the Minor Occultation (874–941 CE), during which he communicated through deputies, and the Major Occultation (since 941 CE), which continues until his reappearance.

Born: 869 CE, Samarra, Iraq
Status: In occultation since 874 CE
Title: Al-Mahdi (The Guided One)
